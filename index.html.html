Client · Kumba Iron Ore Limited

The
Problem

Kumba's value chain depends on three interconnected parts — two mines and one external logistics corridor — that cannot see each other in real time.

Kumba Iron Ore faces a lack of an integrated, real-time digital system connecting Sishen Mine, Kolomela Mine, and Transnet's logistics corridor — resulting in poor coordination, production–logistics misalignment, and lost value.

The Operating Environment

A System That Cannot See Itself

Kumba operates large-scale iron ore mining through Sishen and Kolomela mines, with all export moving through Transnet's rail and port corridor to Saldanha Bay. These three components generate significant operational and financial data — but that data lives in separate systems, owned by separate organisations, with no shared real-time visibility.

The Consequence

Disconnection Costs Value

When Transnet's rail is disrupted, both mines continue producing — loading trains into a corridor that cannot move. When a stockpile builds, no system triggers a production adjustment. Each hour of misalignment is lost revenue. The CIO is directly accountable for a system he cannot currently see in full.

Production at the mines is not automatically adjusted when rail capacity is constrained by Transnet

Stock levels are managed from three separate data sources, creating overstocking or understocking risk

Governance and audit reporting relies on fragmented, manually reconciled information

The 2026 UHDMS transition at Sishen adds a fourth layer of complexity — contractor data, project timelines, and cost tracking — with no integrated home

Phase 3 · The Solution Concept

Kumba Operations
Governance Dashboard

A real-time integrated governance platform connecting Sishen Mine, Kolomela Mine, and the Saldanha rail corridor — giving every decision-maker a single, live view of the entire operating system.

The Salcron solution is a unified, real-time governance dashboard deployed across Kumba's operating system. It integrates production data from both Sishen and Kolomela, logistics throughput from the Transnet-managed Saldanha corridor, safety and equipment status, sustainability metrics, and executive-level alerts — all in one platform.

The dashboard is designed for three user tiers: executive leadership who need instant situational awareness, operations managers who need mine-level production and equipment data, and governance teams who need automated audit trails, ESG tracking, and compliance reporting.

Rather than replacing Kumba's existing systems, the platform acts as the integration layer — pulling data from current sources and presenting it in a unified, structured format that eliminates the manual reconciliation that currently drives both delay and error.
